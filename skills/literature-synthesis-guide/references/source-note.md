# Literature Synthesis

## Purpose

Generic workflow for synthesizing literature into a structured, evidence-grounded summary.

Use placeholders below and adapt the evidence corpus to the active project context.

## Inputs To Confirm

- Research question: `<question>`
- Scope: taxa, geography, period, methods, or discipline boundaries
- Source corpus: uploaded files, reference manager export, search results, or user-provided papers
- Output format: annotated bibliography, table, narrative synthesis, slide outline, or manuscript section

## Workflow

1. Define inclusion and exclusion criteria.
2. Separate direct evidence from background context.
3. Extract study metadata, methods, sample size, variables, results, and limitations.
4. Group papers by question, method, evidence type, or conceptual theme.
5. Identify consensus, disagreement, and evidence gaps.
6. Write conclusions with citation support and uncertainty language.
7. Keep claims traceable to specific papers.

## Output Checklist

- Corpus list
- Evidence extraction table
- Thematic synthesis
- Key limitations
- Open questions
- Citation-ready summary

## Source Policy

Do not invent citations. Use only provided papers, verified sources, or explicitly searched references when browsing/search is requested.
